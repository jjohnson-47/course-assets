Section 12.1: Vector-Valued Functions

Exploring parametric curves through the lens of vector analysis

Aurora Connection ✨ — Just as the aurora traces the invisible paths of Earth's magnetic field, vector-valued functions reveal the hidden structure of motion in space. In this section, we'll explore how parametric equations illuminate the patterns of curves and motion in three dimensions.

Section Overview

In this section, we study vector-valued functions, extending the concept of real-valued functions to those that output vectors. This allows us to describe movements and changes in space, providing a crucial framework for analyzing motion in physics and engineering.

Key Concepts in This Section

  • Definition of Vector-Valued Functions: A vector-valued function is expressed as $\mathbf{r}(t) = \langle f(t), g(t), h(t) \rangle$, where $f$, $g$, and $h$ are real-valued functions.
  • Evaluating and Graphing: Evaluating involves computing each component; the graph is the set of all terminal points with initial points at the origin.
  • Algebra of Vector-Valued Functions: Operations such as addition, subtraction, and scalar multiplication are performed component-wise.
  • Displacement: The displacement vector from $t = a$ to $t = b$ is given by $\mathbf{r}(b) - \mathbf{r}(a)$.
  • Average Rate of Change: For $\mathbf{r}(t)$, the average rate of change on $[a,b]$ is $\frac{\mathbf{r}(b) - \mathbf{r}(a)}{b - a}$.
